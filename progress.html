The Technology Behind Our System

Our Smart Water Quality Management System (SWQM) monitors key water parameters—pH, temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, ammonia, and water level—to maintain ideal tank conditions for Cream Dory. Powered by Arduino and ESP32, the system provides real-time data through a dashboard and LCD display, helping farmers respond quickly to changes in water quality. This automated approach promotes healthier fish, better growth, and more efficient aquaculture practices.

Key Features

Real-Time Monitoring

Continuous tracking of hypothalamus activity through non-invasive sensors provides instant data on fish wellbeing.

  • Tracks stress levels and feeding responses
  • Monitors environmental adaptation
  • Detects early signs of health issues

Data-Driven Decisions

Our AI-powered analytics system processes hypothalamus data to recommend optimal farming conditions.

  • Automated feeding schedule adjustments
  • Water quality parameter optimization
  • Growth prediction modeling

Benefits of Our Technology

Stable Water Quality Monitoring

Ensures stable and consistent water quality through continuous automated monitoring.

Faster Response & Less Manual Work

Reduces manual checking, allowing faster response to changes in tank conditions.

Healthier & Faster-Growing Cream Dory

Promotes healthier and faster-growing Cream Dory through improved water management.



Phase 1 – Research & Planning

We began by identifying the challenges faced by local Cream Dory farmers, especially the difficulty of manually checking water quality. We researched ideal water parameters—pH, temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, ammonia, and water level—and studied the current state of Pangasius farming in the Philippines.


Key Activities:

  • Understanding sensor behavior
  • Identifying suitable microcontroller
  • Planning system workflow and features

Phase 2 – System Design

We designed the overall structure of our Smart Water Quality Management System. This included selecting components such as the Arduino, ESP32, sensors, and Recirculating Aquaculture System (RAS). We also drafted the wiring layout and tank monitoring setup.


Components Designed:

  • Wiring diagram
  • Sensor placement layout
  • Microcontroller and power design

Phase 3 – Prototype Development

Our team assembled the hardware prototype and programmed the microcontrollers. All sensors were connected and tested individually to ensure they could measure water parameters accurately.


  • Building tank frame
  • Installing sensors
  • Connecting wiring

Phase 4 – Dashboard & Display Integration

We integrated our system with a web-based dashboard and LCD display to show real-time sensor readings. This allows users to view data instantly, receive alerts, and monitor tank conditions easily.


  • Setting up Blynk widgets
  • Connecting ESP32 to dashboard
  • Displaying real-time data

Phase 5 – Testing & Calibration

We began cultivating Cream Dory in early November to observe how the system performs in real tank conditions. Throughout this period, we monitored the stability of the automated sensor readings and checked the harvest weight of the Pangasius to track growth progress. Instead of manual testing, we validated our system through real-time observation and interviews with local farmers, comparing their experiences with the patterns detected by our automated monitoring.


  • Calibrating sensors
  • Testing water levels
  • Fixing reading delays

Phase 6 – System Evaluation & Improvements

Next, we will evaluate the performance of the system during extended operation. Based on the results, we will refine the coding, improve sensor stability, and enhance the dashboard interface for better user experience.


  • Performance results
  • Issues found & fixes
  • Final improvements
